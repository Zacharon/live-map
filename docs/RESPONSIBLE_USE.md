# Responsible Use

Live Map is a public-information situational-awareness tool. It helps people browse source-attributed public event records, provider status, and source metadata. It is not an emergency, life-safety, dispatch, military, legal, financial, insurance, travel, compliance, or professional-advice system.

## Data Limits

Live Map data can be:

- Delayed.
- Incomplete.
- Duplicated.
- Wrong.
- Stale.
- Unavailable.
- Changed by the original source after ingestion.

Users must verify important information with original sources and appropriate authorities. Live Map must not claim complete global real-time coverage.

## Prohibited Uses

Do not use Live Map to support:

- Targeting, harassment, stalking, doxxing, violence, or intimidation.
- Illegal surveillance or evasion.
- Credential theft, malware, exploit development, or unauthorized access.
- Tactical tracking of people, private aircraft, vessels, aid workers, vulnerable groups, or sensitive humanitarian locations.
- Scraping, bypassing, or misusing licensed or restricted providers.
- Claims of verified territorial control, military status, legal status, financial impact, or causality unless the underlying sources support those claims.

## Source And Provider Responsibilities

When using or extending Live Map:

- Preserve source attribution and source links.
- Respect provider terms, licensing, rate limits, and redistribution limits.
- Keep provider credentials server-side.
- Do not expose tokens, private contact emails, OAuth secrets, or paid-service credentials in frontend code.
- Label planned, disabled, fixture, credential-required, delayed, and degraded providers honestly.
- Surface provider degradation instead of hiding it behind successful-looking UI.
- Keep discovery leads, observations, and confirmed events distinct.

## Privacy And Safety

Avoid unnecessary personal data. Generalize or suppress sensitive humanitarian, health, aviation, maritime, cyber, and conflict-related details when precision could increase harm.

Live Map should remain cautious, source-transparent, and honest about uncertainty.
