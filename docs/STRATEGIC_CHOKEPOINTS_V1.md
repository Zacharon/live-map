# Strategic Chokepoints v1

## Scope

The browser registry in `src/data/strategic-chokepoints.js` is a maintained set of operational reference areas. It includes Bab el-Mandeb, Hormuz, Suez, Malacca, Singapore, South China Sea, Taiwan Strait, Bosporus, Dardanelles, Panama, Cape of Good Hope, Gibraltar, English Channel, Danish Straits, Mozambique Channel, Lombok, Sunda, Makassar, Turkish Straits, Northern Sea Route, Bering Strait, GIUK Gap, Arctic Circle, Persian Gulf, Gulf of Aden, Red Sea, Arabian Sea, Eastern Mediterranean, Black Sea, Baltic Sea, Gulf of Guinea, Horn of Africa, East China Sea, Sea of Japan, Korea approaches, and Panama's Caribbean approaches.

## Geometry and governance

- Each record has a stable identifier, aliases, controlled type and status, regions, adjacent geography, watched ports/routes, domains, baseline sensitivity, source links, review date, and limitations.
- Geometries are generalized points, lines, or broad polygons for map context and correlation. They are not legal boundaries, navigational instructions, territorial claims, or a statement about sovereignty.
- The registry is reviewed from public International Hydrographic Organization and International Maritime Organization references. Source links establish provenance for the reference set, not real-time operational status.
- Registry status begins as `normal`; condition assessment is computed from current event evidence and shown separately.

## Public-use boundary

The feature is public-information awareness only. It does not provide route planning, vessel tracking, insurance, sanctions, emergency, military, or operational advice.
